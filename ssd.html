What is an SSD?

First, let's address an important question. SSDs (Solid State Drives) are data storage devices, similar to traditional hard disk drives (HDDs), that have become very popular in recent years. Unlike HDDs, SSDs do not have moving parts inside, which translates into several advantages. Inside an SSD, we find a printed circuit board (PCB) on which NAND memory chips, the controller, and mainly the data and power connectors are located.

The NAND memory chips are responsible for storing information in an SSD, and the controller manages the operation of the SSD (doing all the heavy lifting), making it the most crucial component for better performance and durability. Finally, the data and power connectors are used to connect the SSD to the motherboard and power supply, respectively.

SSDs are undoubtedly the most popular devices among users, offering storage units that are much faster than conventional HDDs while consuming less power. Mechanical hard drives have been the biggest bottleneck for computers, and although high-speed drives were created, they never achieved the read and write rates of current SSDs. What used to take several minutes to boot the operating system is now a matter of seconds, and the same goes for games and applications.

advantages of SSDs

As we have seen, SSDs lack moving parts inside. This results in completely silent operation and lower power consumption while generating less heat. However, the most significant difference and what has made SSDs the most popular devices is undoubtedly their performance. The absence of mechanical parts makes SSDs much faster than HDDs, allowing Windows to start in less than 10 seconds and programs to open almost instantly. SSDs are also capable of multitasking without a noticeable loss in performance, something that cannot be said for traditional hard drives.

Disadvantages of SSDs

We are already familiar with the significant advantages of SSDs, but the world is not all sunshine and rainbows, and where there are advantages, there are also disadvantages. The first one is a much higher price per GB compared to HDDs. Although the price difference per GB has decreased significantly since SSDs entered the market, it is still relatively high. To give you an idea, a 120 GB SSD costs approximately the same as a 2 TB HDD. However, I personally advise making the effort because it is a highly recommended purchase.

Recommendation

The best SSD type to buy right now are the M.2 NVME drives due to it's high performance and low cost. M.2 is a storage interface that leverages PCI-Express technology, allowing the hard drive to provide a much wider bandwidth compared to the SATA interface used by standard SSDs. The NVMe protocol takes this to the limit, with speeds reaching up to 2500 MB/s.
