Types of RAM

The RAM (Random Access Memory) of a PC is a crucial component responsible for providing temporary storage for data and instructions that the computer's processor needs to access quickly. It is a type of volatile memory, meaning that its contents are lost when the computer is powered off or restarted. RAM operates at high speeds, allowing the processor to rapidly read and write data during program execution. It serves as a temporary workspace where the CPU can store data and instructions currently in use.

Currently there are 2 relevant types of ram, DDR4 and DDR5. These are the diferences whereas DDR4 memory reaches speeds of up to 3,200 MT/s, DDR5 memory starts at a speed of 4,800 MT/s, which means it is 1.87 times faster. It can even reach up to 6,400 MT/s, although it will take a few years to reach this maximum speed. And it is expected to be surpassed eventually. The other big difference is the MHz clock rate that in DDR4 goes from 1600 MHz to 4400 MHz and in DDR5 goes from 4800 MHz to 8000 MHz.
