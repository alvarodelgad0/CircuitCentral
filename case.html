PC Cases

The main purpose of a PC case is to provide a framework for organizing and securing the various components of a computer system. It helps protect the internal components from dust, physical damage, and electromagnetic interference. Additionally, it helps manage airflow and dissipate heat generated by the computer's components through the use of fans and ventilation.

PC cases come in various sizes, shapes, and designs to accommodate different form factors of motherboards, such as ATX, Micro-ATX, and Mini-ITX. They may feature different expansion slots, drive bays, front panel connectors, and additional features like tempered glass side panels, RGB lighting, cable management systems, and tool-less installation mechanisms. The choice of a PC case depends on factors such as the intended usage, available space, cooling requirements, aesthetic preferences, and compatibility with the desired hardware components.

When buying a PC case one of the main factors you should check it's the airflow. That's why we recommend you buy cases with a mesh front for better airflow.
